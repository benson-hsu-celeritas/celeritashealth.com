The Founder

Built by the specialist on the other end of the phone

As a PICU attending in rural America, I've been the specialist that physicians call at 2 AM when a child is deteriorating and they need help now. I know what those calls sound like — the urgency, the limited resources, the clinician doing their best with what they have. I've taken thousands of those calls. CodeBluePediatrics is what I wish every one of those physicians had in their pocket.

The bottleneck isn't knowledge. It's access. The clinical guidance that saves children's lives shouldn't require a transfer line, a callback, or luck. It should be as immediate as texting a colleague. That's what this is — the specialist you had access to at the academic center, available everywhere, all the time.

I didn't build this as a side project. I built it because I've spent my career at the intersection of clinical medicine, health system operations, and technology — and this is the problem I was put together to solve.

Clinical depth. Board-certified pediatric intensivist. I manage ventilators, vasopressors, and cardiac arrests in children. I know which drug doses are life-or-death and which guidelines are actually followed at the bedside — because I follow them. Every safety layer in this system exists because I've seen what happens when clinical information is wrong.

Operational scale. Former McKinsey consultant. Former VP of Enterprise Data & Analytics and Population Health at a major health system, where I led teams building data infrastructure across dozens of hospitals. I understand how health systems buy, implement, and scale technology — because I've been the buyer and the operator.

National leadership. Elected to national leadership in both the American Academy of Pediatrics and the American College of Critical Care Medicine — the two professional organizations that set the standard for pediatric critical care in the United States. When guidelines change, I'm in the room.

Published and peer-reviewed. 75+ peer-reviewed publications. Published in NEJM Catalyst and Harvard Business Review on health system innovation. This isn't my first time making the case that technology can transform how care is delivered — it's the first time I've built the technology myself.

I built CodeBluePediatrics in 20 days between PICU shifts — 75,000 lines of code, 7,809 automated tests, a system that passed a Staff/Principal-level engineering review. The velocity came from 20 years of knowing exactly what a physician needs at 2 AM and exactly what can go wrong if they get the wrong answer. One person understands every line. That's an asset for due diligence, not a liability.

We're not replacing specialists. We're extending their reach.
