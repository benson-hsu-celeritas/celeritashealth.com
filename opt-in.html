About the Service

CodeBluePediatrics is a clinical decision support platform operated by Celeritas Health LLC, a South Dakota limited liability company. The Service provides specialist-level pediatric clinical guidance to licensed healthcare providers via SMS and WhatsApp messaging.

The Service is designed for physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and other credentialed clinicians practicing in settings where pediatric subspecialty consultation is not readily available. When a provider sends a clinical question by text message, the platform returns a structured, evidence-based response with verified citations — functioning like an on-demand curbside consult available 24/7.

How Providers Opt In to Receive Messages

Messaging is initiated exclusively by the healthcare provider. The Service does not send unsolicited outbound messages. Consent to receive messages is obtained through the following process:

1
Provider-initiated contact. A licensed healthcare provider sends an initial text message (SMS or WhatsApp) to the CodeBluePediatrics service number. The provider must have received the service number through an authorized channel: institutional onboarding, direct enrollment with Celeritas Health, or a partnering healthcare organization.
2
Terms of Service presented. Upon first contact, the system delivers a Terms of Service prompt that clearly discloses: (a) the Service is a clinical decision support tool, not a replacement for clinical judgment; (b) all messages are de-identified before AI processing; (c) no Protected Health Information (PHI) is stored; and (d) the Service is restricted to licensed healthcare providers.
3
Explicit consent required. The provider must reply AGREE to accept the Terms of Service and opt in to receiving messages. No clinical responses, surveys, or other messages are sent until this affirmative consent is received and recorded.
4
Service begins. After opting in, the provider may send clinical questions and will receive evidence-based clinical decision support responses via the same messaging channel.

Consent is recorded electronically with a timestamp, consent version identifier, and messaging channel (SMS or WhatsApp). No provider is added to the Service without their own explicit, affirmative opt-in action. Consent records are maintained in accordance with the Company's data retention policies and the Terms of Service.

How to Opt Out

Providers may stop receiving messages at any time using any of the following methods:

  • Reply STOP to any message from CodeBluePediatrics. This immediately unsubscribes the phone number from all future messages.
  • Reply HELP to receive support information and contact details.
  • Email support@celeritashealth.com to request removal.
  • Cease sending messages to the Service. Because messages are reply-based, no formal opt-out is required if you simply stop texting the service number.

After opting out via STOP, no further messages will be sent to that phone number. A provider may re-enroll at any time by texting the service number again and completing the opt-in process from the beginning.

Note: Replying STOP ends all messages, including clinical responses. If you wish to stop only non-essential messages (such as satisfaction surveys) while continuing clinical use, contact support@celeritashealth.com for assistance.

Privacy & Data Handling

The Service implements the HIPAA Safe Harbor method of de-identification (45 CFR § 164.514(b)(2)). All 18 categories of identifiers are automatically removed from inbound messages before AI processing or data storage. Phone numbers are immediately converted to one-way cryptographic hashes (HMAC-SHA256) that are automatically purged within 2 hours. No Protected Health Information is retained.

De-identified usage data is used for service delivery, quality improvement, safety monitoring, and aggregate analytics. The Company does not sell, rent, or share any user data with third parties for marketing or advertising purposes.

For complete privacy and data handling details, see the Terms of Service, Sections 10–13.
